Key Papers & Reading List
=========================

Essential References for Our Research
-------------------------------------

This is a curated reading list for our research areas. Organized by topic.


Molecular Dynamics Fundamentals
-------------------------------

**Classic References**

- Allen, M. P., & Tildesley, D. J. (2017). *Computer Simulation of Liquids*. Oxford University Press.
- van Gunsteren, W. F., & Berendsen, H. J. C. (1990). Computer simulation in protein dynamics. *Accounts of Chemical Research*, 23(11), 357-363.
- Karplus, M., & Petsko, G. A. (1990). Molecular dynamics simulations in biology. *Nature*, 347(6294), 631-639.

**Force Fields**

- Cornell, W. D., et al. (1995). A second generation force field for the simulation of proteins, nucleic acids, and organic molecules. *JACS*, 117(19), 5179-5197. [AMBER]
- MacKerell, A. D., et al. (1998). All-atom empirical potential for molecular modeling. *Journal of Physical Chemistry B*, 102(18), 3586-3616. [CHARMM]


Protein Folding & Dynamics
--------------------------

**Folding Mechanisms**

- Dill, K. A., & MacCallum, J. L. (2012). The protein folding problem. *Annual Review of Biophysics*, 41, 231-257.
- Brunger, A. T., & Karplus, M. (1988). Molecular dynamics simulations of dynamics and stereochemistry of cyclopropane ring opening in electrocyclic reactions. *JACS*, 110(12), 3747-3760.

**Recent ML Advances**

- Jumper, J., et al. (2020). Highly accurate protein structure prediction with AlphaFold. *Nature*, 596(7873), 583-589.
- Lin, Z., et al. (2022). Language models of protein sequences at the scale of evolution enable accurate structure prediction. *bioRxiv*.


Machine Learning Foundations
-----------------------------

**Seminal Works**

- LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y., & Hinton, G. E. (2015). Deep learning. *Nature*, 521(7553), 436-444.
- Bengio, Y., Courville, A., & Vincent, P. (2013). Representation learning: A review and new perspectives. *IEEE TPAMI*, 35(8), 1798-1828.
- Kingma, D. P., & Ba, J. (2014). Adam: A method for stochastic optimization. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1412.6980*.

**Neural Networks**

- Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Hinton, G. E. (2012). ImageNet classification with deep convolutional neural networks. *NeurIPS*, 25.
- Hochreiter, S., & Schmidhuber, J. (1997). Long short-term memory. *Neural computation*, 9(8), 1735-1780.


Deep Learning & AI
------------------

**Transformers**

- Vaswani, A., et al. (2017). Attention is all you need. *NeurIPS*, 30.
- Devlin, J., et al. (2018). BERT: Pre-training of deep bidirectional transformers for language understanding. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1810.04805*.

**Computer Vision**

- He, K., Zhang, X., Ren, S., & Sun, J. (2016). Deep residual learning for image recognition. *CVPR*, 770-778.
- Dosovitskiy, A., et al. (2020). An image is worth 16x16 words: Transformers for image recognition at scale. *ICLR*, 2021.

**Generative Models**

- Goodfellow, I., et al. (2014). Generative adversarial nets. *NeurIPS*, 27.
- Ho, J., Jain, A., & Abbeel, P. (2020). Diffusion models beat GANs on image synthesis. *NeurIPS*, 34.


Graph Neural Networks
---------------------

**GNN Methods**

- Kipf, T., & Welling, M. (2016). Semi-supervised classification with graph convolutional networks. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1609.02907*.
- Gilmer, J., et al. (2017). Neural message passing for quantum chemistry. *ICML*, 70, 1263-1272.

**Molecular GNNs**

- Schütt, K., Rieger, M., & Gastegger, M. (2021). SchNet: A continuous-filter convolutional neural network. *Chemical Physics*, 554, 111382.


Scientific Machine Learning
---------------------------

**ML Potentials**

- Raissi, M., Perdikaris, P., & Karniadakis, G. E. (2019). Physics-informed neural networks: A deep learning framework for solving forward and inverse problems involving nonlinear partial differential equations. *Journal of Computational Physics*, 378, 686-707.
- Behler, J., & Parrinello, M. (2007). Generalized neural-network representation of high-dimensional potential-energy surfaces. *Physical Review Letters*, 98(14), 146401.

**Physics-Informed Learning**

- Perdikaris, P., Karniadakis, G. E., & Raissi, M. (2017). Discovering governing equations from data by sparse identification of nonlinear dynamical systems. *PNAS*, 113(15), 3932-3937.


Reinforcement Learning
----------------------

**Foundations**

- Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). *Reinforcement learning: An introduction*. MIT press.
- Mnih, V., et al. (2015). Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning. *Nature*, 529(7587), 529-533.

**Applications**

- Silver, D., et al. (2016). Mastering the game of Go with deep neural networks and tree search. *Nature*, 529(7587), 484-489.


Transfer Learning & Domain Adaptation
-------------------------------------

- Yosinski, J., et al. (2014). How transferable are features in deep neural networks?. *NeurIPS*, 27.
- Ganin, Y., & Lempitsky, V. (2015). Unsupervised domain adaptation by backpropagation. *ICML*, 37, 1154-1162.


Benchmarking & Evaluation
-------------------------

- Deng, J., et al. (2009). ImageNet: A large-scale hierarchical image database. *CVPR*, 248-255.
- Rajpurkar, P., et al. (2016). SQuAD: 100,000+ questions for machine comprehension of text. *EMNLP*, 2383-2392.
